Cyber-bullying and School Bullying

Anti-Harassment Slogan : Come out of the shadows to avoid a dark life

Mobbing or psychological harassment or mobbing is an abusive conduct which by repeated or systematic gestures, words, behaviors, attitudes aims at or leads to degrading a person's living and/or working conditions.

1 out of 5 students is confronted with cyber-bullying:

6% of college students are victims of cyber-bullying. The use of the Internet, cell phones and social networks means that harassment between students continues outside the school grounds. Cyber-bullying has been rampant since the early days of the Internet, but has become much worse with the advent of social networks. By the year 2019, more than 40% of people under 50 years have already experienced repeated attacks on online social platforms, including 22% of young people aged 18 to 24.

School harassment is frequently accompanied by cyber-bullying:

School bullying is when a student or group of students frequently and repeatedly harasses a peer with aggressive comments or behaviour. Examples of acts that constitute bullying include taunting, bullying, humiliation, insults. How to stop harassment:

Harassment is a serious problem that affects people of all ages and can take many forms. However, the overall goal of harassment is to make another person feel useless by attacking his or her self-esteem. By learning how to deal with harassment, you will be able to stand up for yourself safely, improve your self-confidence and stop it.

Don't react by harassing him. Although it may be tempting to respond by harassing the person who is harassing you, it could make the situation worse.

Try not to be alone. Bullies usually choose their victims based on how easily they can go after them. People who are alone may seem more vulnerable and become easy targets. Try to always stay with friends or other people during the day so you don't become a target for an abuser.
